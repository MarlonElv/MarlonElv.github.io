Key Takeaways & Lessons Learned — Easely I & II
Key Takeaways
01
Total Pile Quantities Deviate Minorly from Preliminary Design
However the types of piles change significantly between preliminary and final — this mismatch drove procurement confusion, go-backs, and inventory counting errors across both sites.
02
Doubling of Seismic Pile Caused Shortage of Heavy Piles
Doubling of the seismic pile contributed to the shortage of heavy piles such as Yellow — a deficit of 4,191 piles in Easely II alone, with Yellow inventory at 4,140 against a massive demand shortfall.
03
Getting Inventory Counts On-Site Proved Extremely Difficult
Accurate pile inventory tracking was not achievable during active production. Piles move continuously during install, making real-time counts unreliable.
04
12 Blocks Impacted in Easely II — Thousands of Go-Backs
As a result of not having the correct material, 12 blocks were impacted in Easely II and added thousands of go-back visits — compounding schedule and labor cost impact across the project.
Lessons Learned & Suggestions
01
PPP Must Be Compared to BOMM and Preliminary Design at Release
The Preliminary Plot Plan needs to be reconciled against the BOMM and preliminary design as soon as it is released — not after procurement has already begun.
02
Mechanical Must Take Control of Material Counting Upon Receiving
Mechanical teams need to own and verify pile counts at the point of delivery — not rely on vendor or logistics counts that may not align with actual received quantities.
03
Leadership Should Personally Count Deliveries Periodically
To increase accountability, leadership should conduct independent counts at the truck — and a separate count by logistics. Two independent counts catch discrepancies before they compound.
04
Inventory Counts Must Happen During Zero-Production Windows
To accurately count piles in inventory and piles needed to finish a job, counting must be done during no production. During production, piles get moved around making it extremely difficult to get an accurate count.
